Our laboratory, LAECO, located in the historic town of Ouro Preto and overlooking the Pico do Itacolomi, one of the most emblematic geographical sites in Minas Gerais, is dedicated to the study of altitudinal aquatic environments. Mainly temporary pools and ponds. Our aim is to discover the biodiversity of aquatic invertebrates, and how they can reconstitute entire populations after the ecosystem has collapsed during a period of drought. How aquatic plants can be these organisms' best allies, and the relationship between size and diversity. Now you are invited to take part in this incredible journey through unique and enchanting landscapes. And to discover the immensity of all altitudinal aquatic environments, their biodiversity and the threats they face. Yes, threats. Although they are relatively remote from human activity, our footprint can reach them. But you'll see how that happens later.
